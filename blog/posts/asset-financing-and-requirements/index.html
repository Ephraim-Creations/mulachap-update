Asset Financing in Kenya: How to Finance Vehicles and Business Equipment

Asset financing helps you purchase a vehicle or equipment while spreading repayment over time. This guide explains lender checks, down payments, and how to structure repayments around cash flow.

Ask on WhatsApp Book a Visit

What is asset financing?

Asset financing is a funding option used to buy an asset such as a vehicle, machinery, or business equipment. The asset is the main security until repayment is completed.

It is useful when you want to keep working capital available instead of paying the full price upfront.

Common assets financed in Kenya

  • Commercial vehicles and personal vehicles
  • Construction and industrial equipment
  • Office equipment and technology
  • Production machinery and specialized tools
Quick tip: Newer assets and clear supplier documentation usually improve approval speed and terms.

Requirements and documents

Personal or business documents

  • National ID and KRA PIN
  • Bank statements (commonly 3 to 6 months)
  • Proof of income or business cash flow evidence
  • Business registration documents if applying as a business

Asset documents

  • Quotation or proforma invoice from supplier
  • Valuation or inspection where applicable
  • Insurance requirements depending on the asset

How lenders decide your limit

  • Ability to repay based on income or cash flow
  • Asset quality, condition, and resale value
  • Down payment amount and supplier credibility
  • Loan term requested and total cost coverage

Step by step process

  1. Needs and affordability reviewMatch asset cost to cash flow and repayment comfort.
  2. Supplier and asset verificationConfirm documents, quotations, valuation, and supplier.
  3. Offer and termsConfirm interest, fees, insurance, and repayment schedule.
  4. Approval and acquisitionAsset is purchased and security is set up until repayment ends.

Repayment planning checklist

  • Choose repayment dates aligned to business inflows or salary
  • Budget for insurance and maintenance of the asset
  • Confirm total cost, fees, and settlement rules
  • Avoid over financing beyond what the asset can produce
