LPO Financing in Kenya: How to Fund a Confirmed Purchase Order

LPO financing helps suppliers fulfill confirmed orders without straining cash flow. This guide explains verification, required documents, and how to avoid delays.

Ask on WhatsApp Book a Visit

What is LPO financing?

LPO financing is funding used to fulfill a confirmed purchase order. The lender or financier evaluates the buyer, the contract terms, and your ability to deliver, then funds procurement or supply costs.

Key requirements and documents

  • Confirmed LPO or signed supply contract
  • Buyer details and verification contacts
  • Supplier quotations and delivery plan
  • Business registration documents
  • Bank statements and cash flow evidence
Quick tip: Clean buyer verification and clear delivery timelines reduce delays significantly.

Common verification checks

  • Buyer legitimacy and payment capacity
  • Contract validity and acceptance confirmation
  • Delivery milestones and penalties
  • Supplier capability and past performance

Step by step process

  1. Document reviewConfirm LPO, contract terms, and buyer verification plan.
  2. Costing and funding structureAlign funding to procurement, delivery, and milestones.
  3. ApprovalVerification completed and terms agreed.
  4. DisbursementFunds released to execute procurement and supply.
